GEMSBOK

The Gemsbok or South African Oryx, is a large antelope in the genus Oryx. It is endemic to the dry and barren regions od Botswana, Namibia, South Africa and parts of Zimbabwe, mainly inhabiting in the Kalahari and Namib deserts, areas in which it is supremely adapted for survival.

Previously, some sources classsified the related East African oryx, or beisa oryx(Oryx beisa), as a subspecies.

The name Gemsbok is from Afrikans, which itself is from the Dutch word of the same spelling, meaning "male chamois", composed of gems ("chamois") + bok("buck, male goat").
